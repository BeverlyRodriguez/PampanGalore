What is Sisig?

Sisig is a sizzling dish that embodies the spirit of innovation and creativity. Originally created as a humble appetizer by the ingenious Kapampangans, the indigenous people of Pampanga, it has evolved over time into a culinary masterpiece enjoyed by locals and visitors alike. Pampanga's Sisig is a celebration of the harmonious blend of flavors, textures, and aromas that make Filipino cuisine so distinctive. At its core, Sisig is made from succulent, well-seasoned, and finely chopped parts of the pig's head, including the ears, cheeks, and snout. These ingredients are traditionally marinated in a flavorful combination of soy sauce, calamansi juice (Filipino lime), vinegar, garlic, and various spices. The marinated meat is then grilled, fried, or broiled to perfection, creating a symphony of smoky and crispy goodness.

What's Special?

What sets Pampanga's Sisig apart?

What sets Pampanga's Sisig apart is the meticulous attention to detail and the array of tantalizing ingredients that elevate its taste profile. Once cooked, the crispy meat is mixed with finely chopped onions, green chilies, and aromatic herbs, which add a burst of freshness and complexity. To further enhance the dish, it is often topped with a raw egg, allowing the residual heat to gently cook it as it mingles with the sizzling meat, creating a creamy and velvety texture.
