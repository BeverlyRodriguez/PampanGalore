What is Tamales?

Tamales, in Pampanga, presents a delightful twist on the traditional Mexican tamale. This Kapampangan version is a delectable combination of rice, meat, and aromatic spices, all lovingly wrapped in banana leaves and steamed to perfection. It is a dish that exemplifies the artistry and creativity of Pampanga's culinary traditions. Pampanga's Tamales starts with a mixture of ground rice and coconut milk, resulting in a smooth and creamy consistency that forms the base of the dish. This mixture is then seasoned with an array of flavorful ingredients, including garlic, onions, ginger, and a combination of spices such as annatto and turmeric, which lend a vibrant hue and depth of flavor to the tamale.

What sets Pampanga's Tamales apart?

What sets Pampanga's Sisig apart is the meticulous attention to detail and the array of tantalizing ingredients that elevate its taste profile. Once cooked, the crispy meat is mixed with finely chopped onions, green chilies, and aromatic herbs, which add a burst of freshness and complexity. To further enhance the dish, it is often topped with a raw egg, allowing the residual heat to gently cook it as it mingles with the sizzling meat, creating a creamy and velvety texture.
