What is Betute Tagak?

Betute Tagak is a local delicacy that takes inspiration from the bountiful rice fields and marshlands of Pampanga. The dish features a rather unexpected and captivating ingredient—stuffed frogs! Yes, you read that right. Betute Tagak is a celebration of the traditional practice of catching and preparing frogs as a flavorful and exotic delicacy. The process begins with meticulously cleaning and deboning the frogs while keeping their distinctive shape intact. The deboned frogs are then stuffed with a flavorful mixture of ground meat, vegetables, spices, and herbs. This stuffing, often a combination of ground pork or chicken, onions, garlic, carrots, and various seasonings, adds a delightful burst of flavors to the dish.

What's Special?

What sets Pampanga's Betute Tagak apart?

Once the frogs are expertly stuffed, they are carefully deep-fried to a golden crisp, resulting in a delectable dish that is visually striking and incredibly tasty. The crispy exterior gives way to a succulent and tender meat interior, showcasing the artistry and skill of the Kapampangan chefs who bring this dish to life.
