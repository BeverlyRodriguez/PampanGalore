What is Tocino?

Tocino is a staple in Filipino cuisine, and Pampanga's version is a testament to the province's culinary mastery. Derived from the Spanish word "tocino," meaning bacon or cured meat, Pampanga's Tocino is a sweet and savory delicacy that offers a tantalizing blend of flavors and textures. This delectable dish traditionally features thin slices of pork or chicken that are marinated in a sweet and tangy mixture, infused with a combination of ingredients such as soy sauce, vinegar, garlic, sugar, and a touch of calamansi juiceâ€”a Filipino citrus fruit. This marinade works its magic, tenderizing the meat and imparting a distinctive sweet and savory profile that is characteristic of Pampanga's Tocino.

What's Special?

What sets Pampanga's Tocino apart?

What sets Pampanga's Tocino apart is the meticulous process of curing the meat to perfection. The marinated pork or chicken slices are carefully allowed to sit and absorb the flavors, resulting in a succulent and flavorful bite. The meat is then typically pan-fried or grilled, further enhancing its tenderness while creating a delightful caramelized exterior that adds a mouthwatering touch of smokiness. Served with steaming garlic rice and a side of fresh tomatoes or atchara (pickled green papaya), Pampanga's Tocino makes for a hearty and satisfying meal any time of the day. Its sweet and savory notes dance on the palate, offering a symphony of flavors that evoke feelings of comfort and nostalgia.
