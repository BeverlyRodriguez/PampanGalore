What is Bringhe?

Bringhe, often referred to as the Filipino version of paella, is a festive and flavorful rice dish that showcases the creativity and ingenuity of Kapampangan cuisine. Rooted in Spanish influences and infused with local ingredients, Pampanga's Bringhe presents a unique twist on a classic favorite. Bringhe is a harmonious blend of fragrant rice, chicken or pork, and an assortment of vegetables, all cooked in a luscious coconut milk-infused broth. This creates a vibrant medley of flavors that tantalizes the taste buds and transports you to the lush landscapes and vibrant fiestas of Pampanga.

What's Special?

What sets Pampanga's Bringhe apart?

What sets Pampanga's Bringhe apart is the thoughtful combination of ingredients and the meticulous layering of flavors. The rice is often tinted with annatto seeds, giving it a warm, golden hue that is both visually appealing and appetizing. The tender chicken or succulent pork is marinated in a savory blend of spices, enhancing the richness and depth of the dish. To further elevate the flavors, a medley of vegetables, such as bell peppers, peas, and carrots, is incorporated, adding a delightful crunch and vibrant colors to the dish. The addition of coconut milk not only infuses the Bringhe with a velvety texture but also imparts a subtle sweetness that complements the savory components perfectly.
