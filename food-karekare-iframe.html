What is Kare-kare?

Kare-Kare is a traditional Filipino stew that showcases the artistry and depth of flavors that define Pampanga's gastronomy. This beloved dish traces its origins back to the pre-colonial era, when the Kapampangans, the indigenous people of Pampanga, developed a taste for combining various ingredients to create a harmonious blend of flavors. Pampanga's Kare-Kare features tender, succulent pieces of oxtail and/or tripe, slowly cooked until they reach a melt-in-your-mouth consistency. What truly sets this dish apart is the rich and velvety peanut sauce that accompanies the meat. The sauce is made by grinding roasted peanuts, rice, and a medley of spices, resulting in a thick and creamy base that imparts a distinct nutty flavor to the dish.

What's Special?

What sets Pampanga's Kare-kare apart?

To complement the oxtail and peanut sauce, Pampanga's Kare-Kare is typically garnished with a colorful array of blanched vegetables such as Chinese cabbage, eggplant, and green beans. These fresh and crisp vegetables not only add vibrant hues to the dish but also provide a delightful contrast to the rich flavors of the stew. In addition to the robust flavors, Pampanga's Kare-Kare often incorporates the use of bagoong, a fermented shrimp paste, as a condiment. This savory and slightly pungent condiment adds another layer of complexity to the dish, offering a perfect balance of flavors when combined with the creamy peanut sauce.
